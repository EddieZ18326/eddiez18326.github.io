SPIRo: Soft Pipe Inspection Robot

SPIRo (Soft Pipe Inspection Robot) is a pneumatically actuated soft pipe inspection robot for the accurate inspection of gas pipes in difficult-to-reach environments. It uses a compliant scissor linkage, McKibben artificial muscle actuators, and magnetic grippers. It achieves a speed of 15 mm/s when crawling on a horizontal surface and 9 mm/s when climbing up a vertical surface. It can also deform its body 60 degrees vertically and 40 degrees horizontally to clear obstacles. The robot‚Äôs origami-inspired grippers support a total weight of 417 grams of extra payload to account for the necessary leak detection sensors and equipment.

Also implemented on the robot is a multimodal deep feature fusion with a deep forest ensemble learning model to improve SPIRo‚Äôs gas leak detection accuracy and redundancy. The model consists of the convolutional layers of three CNNs, the AlexNet, ResNet-50, and MobileNet, and a deep forest ensemble model with all three CNNs. Not only is the ensemble model more accurate, but it is also more reliable due to increased redundancy in analyzing both thermal images and gas sensor data. In addition, the proposed method is also memory efficient, making it suitable for real-time detection applications.
